<p><p>The prescription medicine market is primarily divided into two types: original brand drugs and generic drugs. Original brand drugs are innovative medications developed by pharmaceutical companies, commonly protected by patents, and are typically marketed under a trademarked name. Generic drugs, in contrast, are copies of these original drugs that become available once the patents expire. They contain the same active ingredients, dosage forms, and effects as the brand counterparts but are sold under their chemical names or under a different brand, often at lower prices.</p></p>
