<p><p>The Glass Fiber Reinforced Polypropylene (GFRP) market includes two primary processing types: injection molding and extrusion molding. Injection molding involves injecting heated GFRP material into a mold to create intricate parts, commonly used in automotive and consumer goods due to its precision. Extrusion molding, on the other hand, involves forcing the GFRP mixture through a die to produce continuous shapes like sheets and profiles, widely utilized in construction and insulation applications. Both methods leverage GFRP's enhanced strength and lightweight properties.</p></p>
