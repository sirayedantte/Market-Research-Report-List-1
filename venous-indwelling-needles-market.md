<p><p>The venous indwelling needles market is primarily divided into two types: open indwelling needles and closed indwelling needles. Open indwelling needles are designed for direct access to the venous system, allowing for easy blood sampling and medication administration but posing a higher risk of infection. In contrast, closed indwelling needles utilize a sealed system that minimizes exposure, reducing the risk of contamination and infections. Both types serve crucial roles in medical procedures, emphasizing safety and efficiency in patient care.</p></p>
