<p><p>The viral vaccine cell culture media market comprises two primary types: adherent and suspension systems. Adherent cell culture involves cells growing on a solid surface, typically using flasks or dishes, making it suitable for certain viral vaccines requiring specific cellular interactions. In contrast, suspension cell culture allows cells to grow freely in a liquid medium, enabling larger-scale production and easier scalability for vaccine manufacturing. Both types play crucial roles in the development and production of effective viral vaccines.</p></p>
